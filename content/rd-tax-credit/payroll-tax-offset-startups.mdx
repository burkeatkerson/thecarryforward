A pre-profit startup owes no income tax, so an income tax credit is a deferred asset. Section 41(h) fixes the timing mismatch: a **qualified small business** may elect to apply up to **$500,000** of its research credit per year against employer payroll taxes — real, current-quarter cash for a company running payroll but not yet running profits.

<KeyTakeaway>
  <p>Companies under $5 million in gross receipts, within five years of their first receipts, can convert up to $500,000 of research credit annually into payroll tax savings — elected on Form 6765, delivered through Form 8974 against Forms 941, starting the quarter after the income tax return is filed.</p>
</KeyTakeaway>

## Eligibility: the qualified small business definition

Two conditions, both tested at the entity (and aggregated-group) level:

1. **Gross receipts under $5 million** for the credit year, annualized for short years.
2. **No gross receipts for any taxable year preceding the five-taxable-year period** ending with the credit year. A company electing for 2026 must have had no gross receipts before 2022.

The second condition is stricter than it reads. Gross receipts include *all* receipts — interest on the seed round's money-market balance counts. A company incorporated in 2020 that earned $400 of bank interest that year started its five-year clock then, and its window may close before meaningful QREs exist. The window is a hard five years of receipts, not five years of elections; the election itself can be made in at most five taxable years. Tax-exempt organizations are ineligible, but partnerships, S corporations, and C corporations all qualify. The QRE side of the claim is built the same way as anyone else's — see [qualified research expenses](/rd-tax-credit/qualified-research-expenses).

## The cap and the two payroll taxes

The Inflation Reduction Act doubled the cap for tax years beginning after 2022: up to **$250,000** may be applied against the employer share of Social Security tax (6.2%), and up to another **$250,000** against the employer share of Medicare tax (1.45%). The split matters operationally — a company with a modest payroll can exhaust its employer Social Security liability before exhausting the credit; the excess elected amount simply carries to later quarters rather than being lost.

Only the employer share is offset. Employee withholding, federal income tax withholding, and FUTA are untouched, and the offset never makes a Form 941 negative — unused amounts roll forward quarter to quarter indefinitely.

## Mechanics: Form 6765 to Form 8974 to Form 941

The sequence runs across two filing systems:

1. **Elect on Form 6765**, attached to a *timely filed original* income tax return (including extensions). The election cannot be made on an amended return, except under limited relief the IRS provided for early years of the regime. The elected amount cannot exceed the credit actually computed — and the [Section 280C reduced-credit election](/rd-tax-credit/how-to-calculate-the-rd-credit) interacts here, since most loss companies skip the 280C election and elect the payroll offset on the gross credit.
2. **Attach Form 8974** to the quarterly Form 941, beginning with the **first quarter that begins after the income tax return was filed**. File the 2025 Form 1120 in March 2026, and the offset first appears on the Q2 2026 Form 941; file in April, and it waits for Q3.
3. **Reduce deposits.** The credit is applied against the liability as it accrues; companies on semiweekly deposit schedules can reduce deposits in anticipation, and payroll providers now handle Form 8974 routinely — but the provider must be told, and a mid-year provider switch is a known place where elected credits get stranded.

## When it is not worth it

The offset does not suit everyone. A company already profitable enough to use the credit against income tax gains nothing from the election. A company whose receipts history fails the five-year test — including acquired companies inheriting a target's receipts history under the aggregation rules — is simply ineligible, and electing anyway creates a payroll tax underpayment. And a company with trivial QREs should weigh the study cost against a small credit, as with any claim. Unused amounts not elected against payroll remain ordinary credits, carried forward under [Section 39](/rd-tax-credit/rd-credit-carryforward-rules) for the profitable years ahead.
